Most Australian cities are located on the coast and in particular the south eastern coast between Melbourne and Brisbane. Around 80% of the Australians live between those cities which also includes the largest city Sydney, the capital Canberra, Gold Coast, Newcastle, and Wollongong.

Great Barrier Reef

Did you know that the Great Barrier Reef is the only living thing visible from space? Cairns is the most famous city to begin a trip out to the reef but Townsville, Port Douglas and Airlie Beach all have great beaches and various tours that can take you out to enjoy the reef. Snorkelling and diving amongst the colourful fish and turtles that make the Great Barrier Reef their home is sure to be a highlight of any trip to Australia! The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system composed of over 2,900 individual reefs and 900 islands stretching for over 2,300 kilometres (1,400 mi) over an area of approximately 344,400 square kilometres (133,000 sq mi).

Animal Species

Approximately 90% of the animals native to Australia are found nowhere else, including the kangaroo, koala, echidna, dingo, platypus, wallaby and wombat. Australia has almost 400 mammal species and about 140 species of marsupials. Marsupials are animals that carry their young in their pouches. Half of the birds that live in Australia, can only be found there.
